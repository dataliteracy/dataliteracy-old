Data Literacy for Prosperous Nepal

Nepal Data Literacy Program is designed to catalyze stronger data-driven decision-making by government and non-government actors (mass media, civil society, and academia) through targeted Data Literacy Workshop to help Nepal achieve its development goals.

The program comprises a 100 hour modular, customizable pedagogy to support both technical skills-building and efforts to enhance a ‘culture of data use’ among Nepalis.

#datafornepal

Expected Outcomes

ACADEMIA

Members of academia will be expected to initiate a process to work with the World Bank and partners to update their existing relevant courses to adapt materials from the training or develop non-credit introductory courses when applicable.

CSO AND MEDIA

Members of CSOs and media will provide trainings to their colleagues inside their orgs and potentially create/lead data-driven projects with the technical support from the World Bank and partners.

PARTICIPANTS

Participants will publish data-driven analysis/blogs on topics related to Sustainable Development Goals in Nepal.

COMMUNITY

Curriculum developed through this course will be published in an open source format to be adapted and used for others to increase data literacy capacity.


June-July 2019 Units

Introduction to the Web & Spreadsheet Basics

The unit covers topics on the different components of the Web, importance of using spreadsheets, creating and working with spreadsheets, and performing basic calculations in spreadsheets.

Intro to Data Fundamentals and Finding Data Online

The unit covers topics on data-driven decision making, types of data, data visualizations, different formats of data, finding data online and navigating national databases.

Obtaining, Wrangling and Analyzing Messy Data

The unit covers topics on working with messy data, parsing PDFs and other formats, testing the reliability of data, performing EDA, and finding stories in data visualizations.

Making Claims with Data

This unit covers topics on the definition of a claim, explains characteristics and examples of a good claim, discusses ways to assess the validity of a claim, and ways to critically analyze a claim.

Data Driven Decision Making and Storytelling

The unit covers topics on ways to use data to craft broad context and audience relevant conclusions, overall process of story telling from coming up with a hypothesis to collecting data to cleaning data to performing analysis and making good claims.


August-September 2019 Units

Introduction to Probability and Inferential Statistics

This unit covers topics on probability theory, theoritical probability and experimental probability, compound probabilities, conditional probabilities, inferential statistics and correlation and regression.

Introduction to Python and Machine Learning

This unit covers topics on Google Colab for data analysis and machine learning, introduction to Pandas and Seaborn, introduction to machine learning and creating simple machine learning models for regression and classification.

Basic Dataviz with Tableau

This unit covers topics on basic data visualization with Tableau including understanding data, organizing it, and presenting it in formats and visualizations that makes sense to people who use it to make a real impact with data.

Leading a Data Driven Project

This unit covers topics on data collection, planning data driven projects and setting up the team, survey methodologies and collecting data by leveraging a mobile device.

Leadership and Design

This unit covers topics on design thinking and it's application, use of big data in design thinking, servant leadership, presentation skills and the characteristics of a good presentation.
