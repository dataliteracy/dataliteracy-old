Data literacy to enhance data-driven decision-making

Nepal Data Literacy Program is designed to catalyze stronger data-driven decision-making by government and non-government actors (mass media, civil society, and academia) through targeted Data Literacy and data systems strengthening. The program comprises a modular, customizable pedagogy to support both technical skills-building and efforts to inaugurate a ‘culture of data use’ among targeted stakeholders.

Data, more specifically, government data can play an instrumental role in helping Nepal government advance its development objectives and its aim of achieving double-digit growth rate, foster partnership among various government agencies, and enable and environment for robust tracking and monitoring of progress.

Expected Outcomes

ACADEMIA

Members of academia will be expected to initiate a process to work with the World Bank and partners to update their existing relevant courses to adapt materials from the training or develop non-credit introductory courses when applicable.

CSO AND MEDIA

Members of CSOs and media will provide trainings to their colleagues inside their orgs and potentially create/lead data-driven projects with the technical support from the World Bank and partners.

PARTICIPANTS

Participants will publish data-driven analysis/blogs on topics related to Sustainable Development Goals in Nepal.

COMMUNITY

Curriculum developed through this course will be published in an open source format to be adapted and used for others to increase data literacy capacity.


Course Units

Introduction to the Web & Spreadsheet Basics

The unit covers topics on the different components of the Web, importance of using spreadsheets, creating and working with spreadsheets, and performing basic calculations in spreadsheets.

Intro to Data Fundamentals and Finding Data Online

The unit covers topics on data-driven decision making, types of data, data visualizations, different formats of data, finding data online and navigating national databases.

Obtaining, Wrangling and Analyzing Messy Data

The unit covers topics on working with messy data, parsing PDFs and other formats, testing the reliability of data, performing EDA, and finding stories in data visualizations.

Making Claims with Data

This unit covers topics on the definition of a cliam, explains characteristics and examples of a good claim, discusses ways to assess the validity of a claim, and ways to critically analyze a claim.

Data Driven Decision Making and Storytelling

The unit covers topics on ways to use data to craft broad context and audience relevant conclusions, overall process of story telling from coming up with a hypothesis to collecting data to cleaning data to performing analysis and making good claims.
